## Original passage

> Our multi-agent orchestration layer unlocks unprecedented enterprise knowledge activation by synergizing retrieval-native cognition, adaptive reasoning pathways, and context-aware action loops across heterogeneous information surfaces.

## What problem is this passage trying to address?

It is trying to say what the product actually helps an organization do with its knowledge and systems.

## What phenomenon is it pointing to?

It points to a common SaaS pattern: a product uses inflated AI language to describe a system that searches information, reasons over it, and triggers actions in different tools.

## Main claims

- C1: The product coordinates multiple AI agents.
- C2: It helps users find and use company knowledge.
- C3: It can reason over context and take actions across different systems.

## Faithful rewrite

This product uses multiple AI agents to search company information, work through tasks, and take actions across different tools.

Its main promise is that it helps teams make better use of knowledge that is currently scattered across documents, apps, and systems.

## Explanation layer (optional)

In plain terms, it is saying:

- we can look across many information sources
- we can decide what matters for the current task
- we can do something with that result, not just show a search page

## Critique layer (optional)

### What is valuable here

The original probably does point to a real architecture: retrieval, reasoning, and action are three distinct pieces.

### What is overstated or hard to enter

Almost every important noun is padded with prestige language: "unprecedented," "activation," "synergizing," "pathways," and "surfaces." That language increases status but lowers understanding.
