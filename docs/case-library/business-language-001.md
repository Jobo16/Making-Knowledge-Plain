## Original passage

> To remain competitive in an increasingly dynamic market environment, organizations must operationalize cross-functional alignment and embed a culture of agile value creation at every layer of the business.

## What problem is this passage trying to address?

It is trying to answer how a company should organize itself when markets change quickly.

## What phenomenon is it pointing to?

It points to a familiar management pattern: companies use broad abstract language to say that teams need to coordinate better and respond faster.

## Main claims

- C1: Markets are changing quickly.
- C2: Companies need better coordination across teams.
- C3: Companies need to respond faster and create useful results more consistently.

## Faithful rewrite

If a company wants to keep up in a fast-changing market, its teams need to work together better and make useful decisions faster.

This does not just apply to leadership. It has to show up across the whole business.

## Explanation layer (optional)

In practice, this usually means things like:

- product, sales, and operations sharing the same priorities
- decisions moving faster between teams
- less time lost to internal friction

## Critique layer (optional)

### What is valuable here

The original is pointing to a real problem: many companies are too slow and too fragmented.

### What is overstated or hard to enter

Phrases like "operationalize cross-functional alignment" and "agile value creation" sound strategic, but they hide ordinary claims that could be stated directly.
