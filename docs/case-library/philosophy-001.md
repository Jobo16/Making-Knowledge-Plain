## Original passage

> The subject does not encounter power as an external object alone, but as a distributed structure that precedes and formats the field of intelligibility through which the subject can recognize itself as a subject in the first place.

## What problem is this passage trying to address?

It is trying to explain how power shapes people before they even become fully aware of themselves as independent individuals.

## What phenomenon is it pointing to?

It points to a situation where the rules, categories, and expectations of a society shape how people think, speak, and understand themselves.

## Main claims

- C1: Power is not only something imposed from outside.
- C2: Power also works by shaping the framework through which people interpret themselves.
- C3: A person becomes legible to themselves within a pre-shaped social structure.

## Faithful rewrite

Power is not just something outside us telling us what to do.

It also works earlier and more deeply than that.

It helps shape the language, categories, and expectations through which we understand who we are. That means a person does not first become a fully independent self and then meet power from the outside. Part of what makes that person understandable, even to themselves, has already been shaped by the social world they entered.

## Explanation layer (optional)

A simpler way to picture it is this:

Before you even start making your own choices, you have already entered a world with ready-made ideas about what counts as normal, successful, respectable, masculine, feminine, educated, or deviant. Those ideas do not just control behavior from the outside. They also help build the lens through which you see yourself.

## Critique layer (optional)

### What is valuable here

The passage is useful because it shifts the discussion from obvious command-and-obedience models of power to deeper forms of shaping.

### What is overstated or hard to enter

The original sentence is so abstract that it hides its own point. The insight is real, but the language makes the reader decode the sentence before they can test the idea.
